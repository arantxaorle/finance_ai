Diversification is one of the most important principles in investing.
Instead of putting all your money into one company or one type of asset, you spread investments across different sectors and asset classes (like stocks, bonds, real estate, or ETFs).

This helps reduce risk because:

If one investment performs poorly, others may offset the loss.

It stabilizes returns over time and protects your portfolio during market downturns.

Example:

If you invest only in technology stocks, a tech market crash could cause major losses.
But if you also hold bonds, healthcare stocks, and index funds, your overall risk is lower.

üì¶ 4. ETFs and Index Funds

ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds) are baskets of assets (stocks, bonds, commodities, etc.) that trade on exchanges like stocks.
They allow investors to gain exposure to a wide range of assets with a single purchase.

Benefits of ETFs:

Diversification

Lower fees than mutual funds

Liquidity (you can buy or sell during market hours)

Example:

The S&P 500 ETF (ticker: SPY) tracks the performance of the 500 largest U.S. companies.
By buying one share of SPY, you‚Äôre indirectly investing in all 500 companies.
